### Master Capstone Project: ROAR + FlOW
#### 09/2021~Present (Expected in 05/2022)
➢ Making AI autonomous driving vehicles to interface with FLOW traffic control infrastructure to improve safety and efficiency of urban transportation with different percentages of AI vehicles in traffic, from 1% in the near future to 100% in the eventual future.\
➢ Developing new reinforcement learning solutions to illustrate strategies to integrate future AI vehicles in legacy urban traffic control systems and further improve safety and efficiency by controlling the transportation infrastructure.\

### Graduation Thesis: Spatio-temporal impact of COVID-19 on Taxi travel demand in New York City
#### 12/2020~06/2021
➢ Explored the difference of taxi demand patterns before and after the pandemic by means of global spatial autocorrelation analysis and Geographically and Temporally Weighted Regression (GTWR).\
➢ Summarized empirical taxi behaviors from GTWR models for future pandemic scenarios.\
➢ Adapted statistical models and the associated analytical process to other types of vehicles.\
Tutor: Associate Prof. Shen Zhang, the vice director of Department of Traffic Information and Control Engineering, Harbin Institute of Technology\
[Project code](https://github.com/Jerry-zpl/Spatio-temporal-impact-of-COVID-19-on-Taxi-travel-demand-in-New-York-City)

### Spatio-Temporal Modeling of Parking Demand Prediction Based on INLA Theory
#### 05/2019~Present
➢ Introduced Integrated Nested Laplace Approximation (INLA) theory to analyze and predict the parking demand unprecedently and compared with other models (including AI or deep-learning methods and general regression methods).\
➢ Depicted explicitly the relationship between dependent variables and independent variables in the INLA regression model in contrast to the big-data based models.\
➢ Achieved higher efficiency by Gaussian Markov Random Fields and sparse matrix compared with the traditional Bayesian inference method.\
Tutor: Associate Prof. Shen Zhang, the vice director of Department of Traffic Information and Control Engineering.

### Road Condition Information Sharing System (RCISS) Based on Vehicle Self-Perception
#### 10/2018~10/2019
➢ Developed independently a system to obtain data by GPS and sensors on the car, transmit data by communication module, match and show the data on the map by python and OpenStreetMap.\
➢ Enabled probe vehicles to collect real time traffic data through sensors and GPS to eliminate the information delay and achieve higher flexibility.\
➢ Proposed a commercialization plan of RCISS to develop an APP in order to facilitate the drivers and or the administrative department to acquire real-time road condition information.\
Tutor: Prof. Hua Wang, the director of Department of Traffic Information and Control Engineering, Harbin Institute of Technology.\
[Project code](https://github.com/Jerry-zpl/Road-Condition-Information-Sharing-System-Based-on-Vehicle-Self-Perception)
